Let $X$ be a Wiener process on $[0,1]$. (We may want to investigate smoother processes such as integrated Wiener later). The Kosambi-Karhunen-Loeve Theorem states that there exists $\{\phi_j\}_{j=1}^{\infty}$, an orthonormal basis of 
$\lt$, and $\{\lambda_j\}_{j=1}^{\infty}$ such that $\lambda_j\asymp j^{-2}$, such that 
$$
X = \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} Z_j \phi_j,\;\;\;\;\;Z_j\sim\mathcal N(0,\lambda_j).
$$
Furthermore, $\{Z_j\}$ are independent.

For $f,g\in\lt$, and $\{\rho_j\}>0$ such that $\sum_{j=1}^{\infty}\rho_j$ converges, define
$$
K(f,g) = \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \rho_j k(f_j,g_j),\;\;\;\;\;\text{where}\;\; f_j=\langle f,\phi_j\rangle,\;\; g_j=\langle g, \phi_j\rangle.
$$
Here $k:\mathbb R\times\mathbb R\to\mathbb R_+$ is certain Marten kernel. This additive kernel will give rise to the Sobolev space on $\lt$ with respect to the Frechet derivative.

Question: Analyze the spectral decay of 
$$
\mathbb E[K_X\otimes K_X] = \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \mu_j\phi_j\otimes\phi_j
$$
where the operator is defined in the RKHS $\mH$ associated with $K$, and $K_X = K(X,\cdot)$.
